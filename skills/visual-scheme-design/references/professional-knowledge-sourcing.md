# Professional Knowledge Sourcing

Use this reference when a residential or spatial decision would benefit from current cases, professional heuristics, product information, research, or safety-critical guidance. Search to improve the logic of a named decision, not to decorate the answer with authority or maximize parameter count.

## Search Objective

Start with the unresolved design question:

- What user routine or spatial problem is unclear?
- What arrangement appears plausible but needs a failure check?
- What case strategy could transfer to this base?
- What material, lighting, or furniture choice would make the spatial logic easier to understand?
- Is there a safety-critical uncertainty that must remain outside the visual proposal?

Do not browse by default when the current base, needs brief, and ordinary design reasoning already support the decision.

## Knowledge Roles

- `case_strategy`: a design move and why it works
- `design_heuristic`: professional reasoning for comparison, not a legal requirement
- `human_use_pattern`: behavior, routine, furniture-use, storage, privacy, or accessibility insight
- `product_constraint`: exact information for a named product or system when it changes the visual scheme
- `research_finding`: relevant evidence with stated scope and limitations
- `safety_boundary`: an issue that online research can identify but not verify on site

Use regulations and standards only when a specific high-risk question requires them. Do not build a jurisdiction dossier or turn ordinary visual design into compliance review.

## Source Priority

Prefer sources that match the claim:

1. primary case documentation, designer or studio material, and reliable drawings for case strategy
2. professional institutions, recognized references, and peer-reviewed research for design heuristics and human use
3. manufacturer technical sheets for exact product constraints
4. current government or regulator sources for a named safety-critical issue
5. reputable technical publications with clear authorship and date
6. image platforms and social content for inspiration only

Search summaries and mood images may locate an idea but are not sufficient evidence for why it works.

## Case Strategy Extraction

Convert every useful case into a transferable record:

```yaml
strategy_id: case-strategy-01
source_title: exact title
source_url: canonical URL
observed_move: what changed spatially
problem_solved: what use problem it addresses
why_it_works: adjacency, circulation, furniture, storage, privacy, light, or perception logic
conditions: what must be true for the move to work
failure_modes: how the same move commonly becomes implausible
current_plan_mapping: relevant room, zone, or object IDs
use_in_option: 方案 A
visual_proof: what the final output must visibly demonstrate
confidence: high | medium | low
```

Do not copy case geometry or let a reference image override the locked base. Extract the move, conditions, and failure modes; then translate them into option operations and a scheme logic manifest.

## Search Procedure

1. State the decision gap and why it affects perceived feasibility.
2. Search for one relevant strategy, failure pattern, or exact product fact.
3. Prefer primary sources and inspect the underlying page, drawing, or data.
4. Extract the smallest transferable conclusion.
5. Record applicability and failure conditions.
6. Map the conclusion to the current base, option, and `visual_proof`.
7. Keep fact, inference, and recommendation separate.

Stop when the decision is supported. Do not collect a large generic knowledge pack during a live project.

## Safety Boundary

Require project evidence or qualified confirmation for structural status, demolition feasibility, fire strategy, gas work, electrical loading, waterproofing, drainage, service relocation, and hazardous materials. Online research may identify the concern and help phrase a warning; it cannot verify the site.

Keep these issues as `blocking_unknowns`, alteration-risk notes, or professional-confirmation items. Do not simulate certainty with precise-looking numbers.

## Use In Scheme Logic

- Use a case strategy to strengthen `core_move`, `conditions`, and `visual_proof`.
- Use a design heuristic to compare surviving options, not to create a hard rule.
- Use a human-use pattern to improve routine, furniture, access, or privacy logic.
- Use a product constraint only when the named product materially affects fit or appearance.
- Use safety research to stop or qualify a risky assumption, not to certify it.

When external knowledge conflicts with the locked base or confirmed user routine, preserve the project facts and explain why the external strategy does not transfer.
